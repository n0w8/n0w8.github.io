Before a day in the fields, on board or in the shield wall, a pot of porridge stood over the fire in the North. Oats gave strength and lasting fullness, wild berries brought sweetness and colour - porridge is no modern invention, it is a Northern inheritance. This recipe from **Viking Feast** takes ten minutes and makes any breakfast a little more epic.

![Oat porridge with berries and honey, from above](/images/rezepte/recipe-porridge-2.webp)

## Ingredients

- 2 cups rolled oats (200 g)
- 2 cups milk or water (500 ml)
- 1 pinch of salt
- 2 tbsp honey
- 3.5 oz berries (e.g. blueberries, raspberries)

## Instructions

1. Bring the oats to a boil in milk or water.
2. Reduce to low heat and let simmer for 5 to 7 minutes until thick and creamy.
3. Sweeten with honey and stir in the berries.
4. Serve hot.

> **Fun fact:** Porridge-style dishes were very common among the Vikings - often enriched with wild berries for flavour and vitamins.

## The Skald's Tip

Fresh berries in summer, preserved ones in winter - the Norse knew both. A handful of chopped nuts on top turns this into a warrior's breakfast. And if you like it sweet, try our [honey-baked apples](/en/blog/honey-baked-apples) for dessert.
