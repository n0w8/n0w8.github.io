No drink is as inseparable from the Vikings as mead. It flowed at feasts, sealed oaths and was offered to the gods - in Valhalla, they believed, it flows forever. The best part: making honey wine is astonishingly simple. Three ingredients, a fermentation vessel and a little patience - that is all this recipe from **Viking Feast** asks of you.

![Mead in bottles and a drinking horn](/images/rezepte/recipe-mead-2.webp)

## Ingredients

- 4 cups water (1 liter)
- 1 ½ cups honey (300 g)
- 1 packet wine yeast (modern alternative)

## Instructions

1. Gently warm the water (do not boil) and stir in the honey until fully dissolved.
2. Pour the mixture into a fermentation vessel, add the wine yeast and seal with an airlock.
3. Let ferment for 4 to 6 weeks in a cool, dark place.
4. Bottle and store in a cool location to mature - the longer it ages, the rounder the taste.

> **Fun fact:** Mead was the national drink of the Vikings - served at feasts, rituals, and even offered to the gods.

## The Skald's Tip

The most important advice: patience. Young mead tastes sharp; after three months of aging it turns smooth and golden.
