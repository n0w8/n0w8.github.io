The North knew no sugar - anyone who wanted to bake sweet needed honey. Which is exactly why this cake tastes the way sweetness tasted a thousand years ago: warm, floral, honest. Five ingredients, one loaf pan, no frills. Straight from our cookbook **Viking Feast**.

![Honey cake, sliced](/images/rezepte/recipe-honey-cake-2.webp)

## Ingredients

- 1 ½ cups flour (rye or wheat) - 200 g
- ½ cup honey - 100 g
- 1 egg
- 3 ½ tbsp butter - 50 g (softened)
- 1 tsp baking powder (modern addition)

## Instructions

1. Mix honey, egg and softened butter until smooth.
2. Add flour and baking powder, then stir into a batter.
3. Pour into a loaf pan.
4. Bake at 350°F (180°C) for about 35 minutes, until golden and firm.
5. Let cool and serve.

> **Fun fact:** Honey was the only sweetener available to the Vikings - used not only for mead, but also for sweet dishes like this one.

## The Skald's Tip

Rye flour makes the cake darker and spicier - closer to the original. Brush it with a little butter while still warm and it will gleam like amber. And to see what else the Vikings made from honey, try our recipe for [homemade mead](/en/blog/how-to-make-mead).
