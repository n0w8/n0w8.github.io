If a single dish sums up the cooking of the Viking age, it is this one: the cauldron over the long fire, where meat, cabbage and roots melt into one rich meal over hours. No frills, no show - only depth. The original recipe from **Viking Feast**, written for the biggest pot you own.

![Viking cauldron stew, served in a bowl](/images/rezepte/recipe-cauldron-stew-2.webp)

## Ingredients

- 1.1 lb meat (beef, pork, or lamb) - 500 g
- 10.5 oz vegetables (carrots, leeks, cabbage) - 300 g
- 7 oz root vegetables (e.g. parsnips, turnips) - 200 g
- 6 ½ cups water or broth - 1.5 liters
- Salt, black pepper, 1 bay leaf

## Instructions

1. Cut the meat into cubes and place in a cauldron or large pot with the water or broth.
2. Simmer for 30 minutes.
3. Add the chopped vegetables and root vegetables.
4. Let simmer for 1 to 2 hours, until the meat is tender and the flavours are rich.
5. Season with salt, pepper and bay leaf, serve hot.

> **Fun fact:** The cauldron was the heart of the Viking kitchen - almost every meal, from stews to broth to porridge, was cooked in it.

## The Skald's Tip

This stew lives on variety: the more different roots go in, the deeper the flavour. Cooked over a real fire, it gains a whisper of smoke no stove can imitate. And after a raid like [Lindisfarne](/en/blog/lindisfarne-raid), many a warrior had well and truly earned exactly this bowl.
