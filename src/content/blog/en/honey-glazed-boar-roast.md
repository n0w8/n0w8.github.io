Whenever the sagas describe a feast, a roast steams at the centre of the table. Wild boar was both hunting trophy and festive dish for the Norsemen - and glazed with honey, their "liquid gold", it turns into a meal that makes any modern table feel like a mead hall. This recipe comes from our cookbook **Viking Feast** and is written so it works in any modern kitchen.

![Honey-glazed wild boar roast, carved and served](/images/rezepte/recipe-boar-roast-2.webp)

## Ingredients

- 3 lbs wild boar leg (1.5 kg, substitute pork if needed)
- 3 tbsp honey
- 3 tbsp mustard
- 2 tbsp oil
- 3 onions, roughly chopped
- 3 carrots, cut into chunks
- 1 cup red wine or mead (250 ml)
- Salt, pepper, thyme, rosemary

## Instructions

1. Rub the meat with oil, salt and pepper.
2. Sear on all sides in a roasting pan, then remove.
3. Add onions and carrots, sauté briefly, deglaze with wine or mead.
4. Return the meat, add the herbs, cover and braise at 325°F (160°C) for 2.5 to 3 hours.
5. Mix honey and mustard, glaze the roast 20 minutes before it is done and caramelize under the broiler.

> **Fun fact:** Vikings loved sweet-savory contrasts. Honey was their "liquid gold" - used in food, drink and even medicine.

## The Skald's Tip

For the full longhouse experience, deglaze with [homemade mead](/en/blog/how-to-make-mead) instead of red wine - the sauce gains a subtle honeyed depth. Serve with fresh [barley flatbread](/en/blog/viking-barley-flatbread) so not a drop goes to waste.

Why was the boar sacred in the North? The boar Sæhrímnir was eaten in Valhalla every single night, only to return whole the next day - read more in our article on the [Norse gods](/en/blog/norse-gods).
