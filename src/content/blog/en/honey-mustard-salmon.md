No creature shaped the Northern diet like the salmon. It was smoked, dried, salted - and fresh from the fjord, simply cooked over the fire. Our version from **Viking Feast** pairs it with the two great flavours of the Viking kitchen: honey and mustard. On the table in 30 minutes.

![Honey-mustard crusted salmon, fresh from the oven](/images/rezepte/recipe-honey-salmon-2.webp)

## Ingredients

- 1.3 lb salmon fillet (600 g)
- 2 tbsp honey
- 2 tbsp mustard (coarse or dijon-style)
- 2 tbsp breadcrumbs
- Salt and black pepper

## Instructions

1. Season the salmon fillet with salt and black pepper.
2. Mix honey, mustard and breadcrumbs into a paste and spread it evenly over the salmon.
3. Bake at 350°F (180°C) for about 20 minutes.
4. The crust should be golden and crispy.

> **Fun fact:** Salmon was so valuable that in Iceland it even served as a means of payment. Dried fish accompanied the Vikings on every long sea voyage.
