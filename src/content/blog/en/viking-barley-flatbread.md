Before there were ovens, there were hot stones - and on them the women of the North baked flatbreads from barley, the defining grain of the Viking age. Four ingredients, no yeast, no waiting: this recipe from **Viking Feast** is bread baking in its oldest form. And it works in any cast-iron pan.

![Barley flatbreads, stacked and served warm](/images/rezepte/recipe-flatbread-2.webp)

## Ingredients

- 2 ½ cups barley flour (300 g)
- ½ cup + 2 tbsp water (150 ml)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tbsp honey

## Instructions

1. Knead barley flour, water, salt and honey into a dough.
2. Shape the dough into small, flat rounds.
3. Cook on a hot stone or in a cast-iron pan without oil until both sides are golden brown.
4. Serve warm - plain or as a side to meat or fish.

> **Fun fact:** Barley was one of the most important Viking grains - not just for bread, but also for brewing beer.

## The Skald's Tip

These flatbreads are the born side dish: they mop up the sauce of a [honey-glazed boar roast](/en/blog/honey-glazed-boar-roast) and carry honey and butter at breakfast. Bake them over real embers and you get the subtle smoky notes that once drifted through every longhouse.
