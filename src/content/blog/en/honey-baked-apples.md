In Norse mythology, the goddess Idun guards the apples of youth - whoever eats them does not age. Our version sadly cannot promise that, but it fills the house with a scent of honey, cinnamon and toasted nuts that makes life feel longer anyway. A dessert from **Viking Feast**, ready in 25 minutes.

![Baked apples with melting honey](/images/rezepte/recipe-baked-apples-2.webp)

## Ingredients

- 4 tart apples (e.g. Boskoop or Granny Smith)
- 4 tsp honey
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- 1 handful of nuts or raisins (or both)

## Instructions

1. Core the apples.
2. Fill each apple with honey, nuts and/or raisins.
3. Sprinkle with cinnamon.
4. Bake at 350°F (180°C) for about 20 minutes, until soft.
5. Serve warm - optionally with a little cream.

> **Fun fact:** To the Vikings, apples symbolised fertility and eternal life - the goddess Idun guarded the apples of youth.

## The Skald's Tip

Tart apples keep the balance against the sweet honey - the sharper the apple, the better the result. Read the full story of Idun and the gods of Asgard in our article on the [Norse gods](/en/blog/norse-gods).
